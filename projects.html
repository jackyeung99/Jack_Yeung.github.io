Efficient Inference of Rankings From Multi-Body Comparisons [In Review]

This paper introduces a generalization of the efficient iterative step developed by Mark Newman of the Bradley-Terry (BT) model, extending its application from two-entity interactions to multi-body interactions. The BT probabilistic model, similar to the Elo rating system used in chess, is a widely used player rating algorithm. Two-entity games involve head-to-head interactions between two teams, players, or entities, such as soccer matches or boxing bouts. In contrast, multi-body interactions encompass scenarios involving more than two entities, such as Formula One racing. To accommodate these higher-order interactions, we employ hypergraphs, a generalization of standard graphs, as the framework to adapt the efficient schema of the BT model for multi-body interactions allowing a quicker estimation of player stength scores.
